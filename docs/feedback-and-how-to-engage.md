## Pick the right channel

## GitHub Issues

Use GitHub Issues for anything that has a reproducible test case or a concrete capability ask.

**Before filing,** scan the open issues for a duplicate and read the [Known limitations](known-limitations.md) page. If your issue is already a known limitation, you can comment with your scenario instead of opening a new issue.

**When you file,** include:

- Container image tag
- Host OS and architecture
- Container runtime and version
- Driver or ORM and version
- Reproduction steps
- Expected behavior
- Actual behavior

The issue templates ask for all of this. The more complete the report, the faster the triage.

## GitHub Discussions

Use Discussions for everything that is not a clean bug or feature request:

- **Q&A:** how do I, what is the right way to, did anyone else hit
- **Show & Tell:** here is what I built, here is a pattern that worked
- **Ideas:** I think the container should, here is a use case we have not seen
- **Announcements:** updates from the team, posted by maintainers

Discussions move slower than the Teams channel but they are searchable and durable. Questions answered in Discussions help every future participant who runs into the same thing.

## Private Teams channel

Customers in this Private Preview are under NDA. The Teams channel is private, access-controlled, and the right place for:

- Real-time conversation
- Questions you do not yet want to put in a public issue
- Quick polls from the team to the cohort

The Teams channel is not a substitute for filing an issue. If something is a bug or a feature request, please file it on GitHub so it does not get lost in chat.

## Office hours

Weekly office hours run for the duration of the Private Preview. The agenda template:

- 5 min: opening, what shipped this week
- 15 min: customer demos (volunteer in the Teams channel ahead of time)
- 15 min: open Q&A
- 10 min: engineering update

Engineering attends. Bring your hardest question.

## Profiling survey

One week after you sign up, you receive a short profiling survey by email and in the Teams channel. It covers stack, deployment environment, scenarios, and use cases. It takes about five minutes and helps us segment the cohort so we can tailor what we ship next.

## Response SLAs

The triage rotation owns these SLAs for the Private Preview:

| Channel                       | First response             | Plan                       |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| GitHub issue (P0)             | 1 business day             | 2 business days            |
| GitHub issue (P1)             | 1 business day             | 5 business days            |
| GitHub issue (P2 or feature)  | 5 business days            | Triaged in the next sprint |
| GitHub Discussion (Q&A)       | 1 business day             | n / a                      |
| Teams channel                 | Best effort, same day      | n / a                      |

If you hit a P0 and we have not acknowledged within one business day, escalate in the Teams channel.
